SYNOPSIS

This is based on the remarkable true life story of Annie Besant (847-1934) A strong orator, energetic activist and idealist, this Englishwoman mixed with the leading politicians and writers of her day. A modern women, a pioneer of the feminist movement yet a deeply spiritual person.

The script covers her journey from a deeply religious and sheltered life to gaining awareness of the poverty and misery of the the poor. By chance, she stumbles into the then popular of spiritualism, secularism and socialism and becomes a well-known activist in the trade union movement and for women's rights. In London, she was a friend of Oscar Wilde and other society people as well as the lover of George Bernard Shaw. But she never quite threw off her spiritual side and, at the height of her popularity, suddenly turned her back on political life and joined Madame Blavatsky, the notorious Russian who founded the Theosophical Society. Annie Besant eventually became President the Society and moved to India. She adopted Krishnamurti, who went on to become a world-renown teacher and philosopher. Annie returned eventually to politics and became the only white women to head the Indian National Congress where she fought alongside Gandhi for home rule.

This is the story of a woman who succeeded against the odds by her power with words and became a beacon for women who followed. Yet she was also a flawed character, lacking humour and misled by men. Right up to her old age she was still searching for a truth that always seem to eluded her but finds some reconciliation in her last years.

Our amazing tale takes us from England to America, India, Sri Lanka and Singapore as Annie pursues her passions. The story offers many opportunities in capturing life and scenes from the Victorian era, in London and the more exotic places. Apart from the roles offered to the three or four actors portraying Annie, there are many cameo performances which will excite actors.

I would hope that funding from England, India and Singapore as well as the USA would assist the financing of production.

CREDITS

GANDHI began to dress in simple white dhobi and started his campaign. As Annie Besant feared it did lead to bloodshed but eventually in 1947 India won complete independence. He remained respectful of Annie despite their differences.

KRISHNAMURTI eventually became a famous and highly- regarded spiritual leader, author and philosopher. He died in 1986.

GEORGE BERNARD SHAW went from a penniless writer to a famous playwright creating plays such as St. Joan, and Pygmalion, which was turned into the hit musical My Fair Lady. He remained a socialist and wrote many books and pamphlets.

OSCAR WILDE. Wrote many plays such as The Importance of Being Ernest and Lady Windermere's Fan and the novel The Picture of Dorian Gray. Despite his fame and brilliance was thrown in jail for a homosexual relationship and emerged a broken and poor man dying in Paris soon after in 1900.

ROBERT STEAD became famous and is considered the father of the tabloid newspapers and investigative journalism. He drowned in the Titanic sinking after heroically helping others to safety.

Hypatia Bradlaugh and Mabel Besant both continued the work of their respective parents becoming activists for their causes.

THE THEOSOPHICAL SOCIETY remains an active world-wide organisation although in numbers has shrunk. The headquarters remain in Adyar, India.

Rev. Frank Besant remained in Sisley until his death in 1917, spending his leisure time collecting parish history. He is buried in St Margaret's Church graveyard.

ANNIE BESANT is still revered in India. There are several street named after her. She had written and spoken eloquently on independent governance for the India while still in London and was a major force in the movement in India years before Gandhi came on the scene. She founded many schools, supported women's rights and promoted both Hinduism and Buddhism, urging Indians to take a pride in their culture. In the West, however, she is largely forgotten. Other people have instead become associated with her pioneering efforts in women's rights, birth control, sex education. Her speeches and campaigns on Home Rule for Ireland and Trade Unions and her success in introducing free school meals and medical services for pupils are not remembered. Had Annie Besant gone into English politics, she would have probably been the first woman Cabinet Minister. She also found time to become a Freemason in Europe becoming the Grand Master in Co-masonry in England. A fine orator, an indomitable organiser and a truly remarkable woman.
